Pompeii – a town with a difference

On your next trip to ltaly, why not visit the fantastic Roman town of Pompeii? In the past, twenty thousand people lived in Pompeii in big houses with big gardens. The Romans built roads in the town and there were many public places like baths, amphitheatres, banks, and markets. There wasn’t any electricity and people got up with the sun. For breakfast they had bread and cheese. People started work very early and stopped at twelve o'clock for a lunch of fish, bread, cake and fruit. In the afternoon people went to watch the gladiators or to the public baths to wash and meet friends. In the evening they often had dinner parties They were an important part of Roman life. But life in Pompeii stopped in 79 AD when the volcano Vesuvius erupted. Today you can see the town exactly as it was nearly two thousand years ago.
